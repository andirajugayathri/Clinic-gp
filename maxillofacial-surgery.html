Our Specialized Surgery Types

Jaw Asymmetry

Jaw asymmetry is typically caused by misaligned jaws, congenital malformations, malpositioned teeth, intra-uterine trauma, or disproportionate jaw development. A simple check to identify if you have disproportionate jaws would be to check the ratios of your face with respect to the upper part (forehead), middle part (nose) and the lower part (lips and chin). Orthognathic or jaw surgery involves surgical correction of these deformities, thereby adjusting and aligning the bite to make the jaws aesthetically perfect.

Temporomandibular Joint Disorders (TMJ)

Temporomandibular joint disorders are a series of complex disorders occurring at the joint that connects the lower jaw to the skull. These are caused by problems and disruption related to the muscle, soft tissue and bone, affecting overall functionality. Severe TMJ disorders increase oro-facial dysfunction and leads to pain and discomfort. Concerns with chewing, clenching of teeth and locking of the jaw joint are some of the discomforts associated. While bite guards and physiotherapy may relieve concerns, in severe cases it requires surgical correction in the joint or a complete joint replacement.

Pediatric Maxillofacial Surgery

The anatomy of a child's facial skeleton differs from the adult anatomy and definitive changes occur as the child grows and develops. Pediatric maxillofacial surgery specially attempts to treat birth deformity, trauma, pathology and oral air-way disruptions in children with a higher understanding of the impact of the disease or injury on the growing facial anatomy. We derive the best of methods to treat the fragile conditions of children without hampering their future growth.

Facial and Cranio-maxillofacial Trauma

We provide services to address and correct different degrees of trauma experienced through accidents and injuries. Facial and Cranio-maxillofacial trauma includes injuries involving the craniofacial region and these can range from being mild injuries to severe life-threatening injuries that require specialized intervention. These reconstructive surgeries include treating maxillofacial fractures, soft tissue injuries of the face, nerve injuries and also complex trauma surgeries to surgically correct improperly treated or untreated post-traumatic defects.

Microvascular Reconstructive Surgery

Restoring face and neck post the abnormal growth and the cancer cells have been treated or removed. Microvascular surgery is a tissue transfer technique that's followed by a skin graft from a donor site of the patient's body, to repair the space left because of the surgical defect. This is performed through a free flap tissue transfer connecting the blood vessels of the trauma part with the ones of the new tissues to ultimately provide a fuller, natural shape and an excellent cosmetic result.
